Designing for 2026 often means going beyond a theme’s defaults – assembling pages quickly, automating bookings, visualizing data, adding motion, mapping locations, organizing media, and handling forms cleanly. 

The plugins below cover those jobs without forcing workarounds: [**Brizy**](#brizy) for visual building, [**Amelia**](#amelia) for appointments and events, [**wpDataTables**](#wpdatatables) for responsive tables and charts, [**LayerSlider**](#layerslider) and [**Slider Revolution**](#slider-revolution) for animated/slider content, [**MapSVG**](#mapsvg) for interactive maps, [**Essential Grid**](#essential-grid) for gallery/grid layouts, and [**IvyForms**](#ivyforms) for forms. Each focuses on practical workflows, responsive output, and steady updates.

Click anywhere on the video to see **MapSVG** live.

MapSVG enables you to create information windows of any complexity.

-   You can create any custom objects you choose. You could create and identify real estate property locations, office locations, or sales representative locations to name but a few. For example, if your company has multiple store locations you can display those locations and accompanying data on a map.
-   You can create multicolored maps where the different colors have different meanings or represent different values.
-   Objects located on a map can also be listed in a directory, filtered by region, and additional information about each object can be displayed.
-   Images can be attached to objects and map regions. Images can be thumbnail, medium, or full-sized.
-   A support chat with AI is integrated into the plugin.
